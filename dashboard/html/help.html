Graph--instruction
What do these graphs mean? & What information can we get from these graphs?

IPO Information graph:


The bubble plot, with IPO price as y axis and IPO data as x axis, illustrates the basic IPO information of companies having low-ball price option that listed on the market. Different colours show whichever market the stock listed and the size of bubble represents the amount of IPO value. When holding the mouse onto the bubble, detailed information about listed date, IPO price and company name will be shown.


IPO stock price / symbol graph:


This breakdown-bar chart focus on comparing IPO price of companies. It clearly show the gap among companies with low-ball price option went to public on 2015. The vertical axis represent IPO price. and the horizontal axis represent the ticket name of a company. Holding the mouse on each bar, you will get the ticket of that company and the public offering price.


Stock amount / symbol graph:


This breakdown bar chart specifically visualize the IPO value of companies in the database. The vertical axis represent value with the unit of Billion(B) and the horizontal axis represent the ticket name of a company. User may put the mouse on a single bar, for instance FDC (FIRST DATA Corporation), and read exact listed value of the enterprise.


Index Comparison graph:


1. Both 2015 listed companies with and without lowball price stock options are in the general trend with the S&P 500. The general moving trend of the market has a strong positive effect on those companies general stock performance trend.

2. Compare index_lowball with S&P 500 and index_withoutlowball, it can be found that companies issued lowball price options had greater growth rate but also higher risk-volatility.

3. Consider the risk and premium of stock investment, invest in lowball price options company put a higher standard on risk control while also may bring investors a higher return rate than market index portfolio.


The closing price trend of four clusters' company graph:


This plot shows the pattern of stock performance of companies in each cluster. Hold the mouse on the curves, users will see the accurate average closing price of clusters. It provides a general insight of risk and growth potential of those companies.
