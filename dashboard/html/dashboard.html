Definition of Lowball Price

Lowball is an offer that is significantly below the seller’s asking price, or a quote that is deliberately lower than the price the seller intends to charge.

Lowball prices on stock options are options allowing stock purchase price significantly below what the shares are really worth—the price that investors would pay for them.

Companies that issued this kind of options before IPO is regarded as lowball price options company and they would be chosen as components of Architecture Intelligence index pool.
